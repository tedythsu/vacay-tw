## Design Context

### Users
Taiwanese office workers — dual context: quick mobile lookups during commute or lunch break, and occasional desktop planning sessions at home. Utility-first mindset; they want the answer fast, not an experience.

### Brand Personality
Clean and no-nonsense. Information-first. Minimal decoration. Think a sharp colleague who already ran the numbers — just show me which days to take off and why. Not playful, not corporate — practical and trustworthy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode is appropriate: daytime work-hour usage context, utility tool, not a leisure app. Theme should feel calm and functional, not flashy. No dark mode for now.

### Design Principles
1. **Data is the hero** — typography and layout should make numbers scannable at a glance, not decorate around them.
2. **Mobile-first, desktop-aware** — the core experience is a phone in one hand. Desktop should not just be a wide phone.
3. **Purposeful color** — every color should carry meaning (red = holiday, yellow = take leave, amber = best pick). Decorative color should be near-zero.
4. **No visual noise** — every UI element must earn its place. Reduce badges, borders, and shadows to the minimum needed.
5. **Taiwanese context** — copy, calendar, and hierarchy should feel locally appropriate, not like a translated global SaaS product.
